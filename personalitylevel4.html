Seeking Treatment

Dialectical behavior therapy (DBT):

DBT is designed for groups and individual therapy, specifically targetting treat borderline personality disorder. DBT uses a skills-based approach, where it teaches individuals how to manage their emotions, tolerate distress and improve relationships.

Schema-focused therapy:

Schema-focused therapy can also be done individually or in a group, where it aims to help identify an individual's unmet needs that have negatively impacted their life patterns. This therapy focuses on helping one meet their needs in a healthy manner and to promote positive life patterns.

Mentalization-based therapy (MBT):

MBT is a type of talk therapy that aims helps one identify/understand their own thoughts and feelings at any given moment. It creates an alternate perspective on any situation and emphasizes thinking before reacting.

Systems training for emotional predictability and problem-solving (STEPPS):

STEPPS is a 20-week treatment that involves working in groups and with the clients' family members, caregivers, friends or significant others, during the treatment. STEPPS is normally an additional therapy to other types of psychotherapy.

Transference-focused psychotherapy (TFP):

Also known as psychodynamic psychotherapy. TFP aims to help one understand their emotions and interpersonal difficulties by developing relationships (specifically between them and their therapist). They then apply these insights to ongoing situations.

Good psychiatric management:

This treatment relies on case management, for it anchors treatment with the expectation of work or school participation. It focuses on the understanding of emotionally difficult moments by having one consider the interpersonal context for their feelings. The therapy occasionally integrates medications, groups, family education and individual therapy.
