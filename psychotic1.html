Psychotic Disorder

Level 1: Signs and Causes

People with psychotic disorders fail to retain a view of reality and experience severe symptoms that usually includes:

  1. Feeling unusually confused, forgetful, on edge, angry, upset, worried, or scared
  2. Yelling or fighting with family and friends
  3. Having persistent thoughts and memories you can't get out of your head
  4. Hallucinations - Hearing voices or seeing things no one else hears or sees
  5. Delusions - believing things that are not true

Doctors don't know the exact cause of psychotic disorders. Possible reasons are:

  1. Some psychotic disorders tend to run in families, which means that the disorder may be partly inherited
  2. Stress, drug abuse, and major life changes
  3. certain psychotic disorders, such as schizophrenia, may also have problems in parts of the brain that control thinking, perception, and motivation.
  4. In schizophrenia, experts believe that nerve cell receptors that work with a brain chemical called glutamate may not work properly in specific brain regions. That glitch may contribute to problems with thinking and perception.
Conditions usually first appear when a person is in his or her late teens, 20s, or 30s. They tend to affect men and women about equally.
