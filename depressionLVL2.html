Depression

level 2: What is Depression?

As stated before, there are multiple types of depression, but it is characterized as a persistent negative, depressed mood that can impair daily life.

It's not just feeling sad.

  1. Major depression is what one might think what depression typically is. It's a dark all-consuming mood, and is treated with therapy and medication most of the time. Thoughts of self-harm and suicide may occur and symptoms must lasts for at least two weeks for a diagnosis.
  2. Persistent Depressive Disorder is characterized as a low mood that lasts for at least two years. While it may not reach the intensity of major depression, sufferers feel low or joyless majority of the time while being able to complete everyday tasks.
  3. Although Bipolar disorder is a form of personality disorder, the low end has similarities to major depression.
  4. Premenstrual dysphoric disorder is the severe extension of premenstrual syndrome. It is sometimes severe enough to disable the sufferer.

It's not just feeling sad.
