Anxiety: What is it & Its Effects

Anxiety is actually pretty complicated:

Anxiety disorders in general are centered around excessive fear/anxiety causing its victim to display behavioral/functional disturbances as a result of the stress it creates. There are many different types of anxiety, each triggered by different situations. Some examples are seperation, selective mutism, social and other phobias, panic, substance-medication-induced, and generalized. They each have different causes and they all have different effects on how those inflicted by them live their lives.

The effects of anxiety can commonly involve:

! - Panic attacks: this is the abrupt onset of intense fear or discomfort that reaches a peak within minutes. Panic attacks leave the victim trembling with short breath, at times they feel like they can't breathe. These attacks last from 20 - 30 minutes and are unpredicatable, leaving people scared of when the next one will occur. NOTE: Anxiety attacks are similar, but they are short lived once the tigger/situation for this attack is gone.
