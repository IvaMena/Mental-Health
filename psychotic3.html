Psychotic Disorder

Level 3: Effects

Because the symptoms of psychotic disorders can be severe, especially when they are not treated, a person can be vulnerable to experiencing a whole host of negative effects.

The following adversities are examples of those that may occur in the event an individual does not receive care for a psychotic disorder:

  1. Difficulty forming relationships with others
  2. Inability to acquire or maintain gainful employment
  3. Financial strain
  4. Social isolation
  5. Worsening of psychotic symptoms
  6. Development of additional mental illnesses
  7. Homelessness
  8. Severe emotional and behavioral disturbances
  9. Difficulties with language
  10. Substance abuse, addiction, or chemical dependency

The likelihood that a person will suffer from additional mental health concerns at the same time as a psychotic disorder is high. Among the various types of disorders that a person can be diagnosed with at the same time as a psychotic disorder, the following are the most common:

  1. Tobacco use disorder and other substance use disorders
  2. Anxiety disorders, including panic disorder
  3. Obsessive-compulsive disorder.
